Before Python: The State of Coding in the 1980s

Before Python, programming was often complex and not very beginner-friendly. Popular languages in the 1980s included:

  • Fortran: Mainly for scientific and engineering calculations.
  • C: Powerful, low-level, but tricky for beginners.
  • COBOL: Used for business applications, very verbose.
  • Basic: Simple for small programs, often used in schools.

Writing code back then often required remembering strict syntax rules and spending a lot of time debugging errors. Computers were slower, and programming environments weren‚Äôt as user-friendly as today‚Äôs IDEs (Integrated Development Environments).

The Birth of Python

In December 1989, Guido van Rossum began working on a new programming language during his time at CWI (Centrum Wiskunde & Informatica) in the Netherlands. He wanted a language that:

  • Was easy to read and write.
  • Had clear and simple syntax.
  • Could be used for a variety of tasks, from small scripts to larger software projects.

Python was officially released in 1991. It quickly gained attention because it combined simplicity with power, allowing beginners and experienced programmers alike to write effective code without frustration.
